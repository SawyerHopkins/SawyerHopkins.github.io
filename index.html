ProteinSim 2015

ProteinSim is a C++ based particle and force management engine which uses abstract classes to be highly extensible to a wide range of integration methods and intermolecular forces. I have developed this engine for my specific work at Kansas State University, but have made the system robust enough for use as a general particle simulation engine.

K-State Nanotech

I have worked for a year with Dr. Chris Sorensen investigating graphene gels created directly via high temperature hydrocarbon detonation. This research has been guided toward investigating the interaction of the gels with organic solvents and polymers.

Michigan Technological University

I have three years experience at Michigan Tech in the field of nano-particle synthesis, with an emphasis on boron nitride nanosheets. My research efforts here were focused on studying potential applications of boron nitride nanostructures for use in heat management systems by the direct measurement and comparison of thermal gradients. To perform these measurements it was necessary for me to design the material testing apparatus as well as develop code to parse and analyze large amounts of temperature measurement data. I left Michigan Tech leading way into initial research on creating commercial applications of boron nitride nanosheets for heat dissipation purposes.

SciLib

SciLib is a numerical analysis and computation engine created using C#, F#, and XNA/MonoGame. This project started as an experiment to explore the logic needed to perform generic N-Space calculations while also learning the oddities of Microsoft's new scientific programming language F#. Later I found having this N-Space computational engine to be very useful for various aspects of my research and course work. Today SciLib is a compilation of various scientific and mathematical tools I have built using this computational engine, and includes basic mathematics, integration, and 3D plotting amongst various other tools. The source code is currently available on my GitHub which is accessible via the social links at the top of the page.
