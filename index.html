About Me

I'm Sawyer Hopkins, a senior at Michigan Technological University. Currently I am pursing my undergraduate degree in physics with a focus on nanotechnologies, solid state physics, and computational sciences. I am a computer and programming enthusiast, primarily working C#. I am familiar with languages including F#, Java, Python, C++, and MATLAB.

Research And Employment

Dr. Yoke Khin Yap Research Group

I have been a nanotechnologies researcher with Dr. Yoke Khin Yap for two years. Over the course of this time I have become very familiar with the chemical vapor deposition process for synthesizing various nanostructures, and have spent most of my efforts in examining the thermal properties of boron nitride nanosheets. My research is currently focused on finding potential methods in which these nanostructures can be used to solve industrial problems of thermal dissipation and heat management.

MTU Teaching Assistant

This is my third year working a teaching assistant in physics department at Michigan Technological University. Over the course of this time I have been a TA for the university wide introductory survey of physics lab (PH1100), and am currently the TA for the electronics laboratory which is part of the physics undergraduate curriculum (PH2230).
